The 58
session

58 is a problem solving methodology to help teams thrive in challenging environments. It combines agile methodology, design thinking and rapid productivity hacks putting talent in the center of the problem solving process.
58 is not a workshop, It’s an intense working session. It differentiates from design thinking sprints and other similar approaches by bringing back what is missing from these sessions, criticism or “Crit” as designers like to call it. It puts teams working as one, without interruptions, under extremely tight deadlines to solve a problem and deliver the actual work.
The methodology finds its origins in REBORRN, a London based digital consultancy. After successfully completing dozens of sessions, they decided to make this framework open source, sharing it with teams around the world making it accessible to everyone.

Use Cases

Strategy mode
If you’ve even taken up the task of creating a business plan, a growth roadmap or a Product’s Strategy, you know that 90% of the work can be done in 3 days with the right people in the room and the lack of distraction. Most teams use 58 Session to turn around a business challenge.
Last minute mode
There are times when not everything goes according to plan, where you find yourself against the clock. Use 58 to set up a task force to work under pressure and reach that deadline. Its unbelievable the volume of work a team can produce just by working with discipline and no interruptions.
Prototype mode
When you have a new product idea you don’t need to spend a fortune and months of work to get a Prototype, a Business plan and a user Growth roadmap. By assembling the right team of experts in less than 3 days you can have a visual prototype to see how it feels like, put together a business case to understand what kind of resources you need, draft a go to market plan to figure how this idea can reach potential customers, and design a concrete product roadmap to plan your next actions. We’ll show you how.

Rules

We see many versions of 58 Methodology working but we ve put together a set of fundamental rules that makes this session efficient. But rules are meant to break. Paradox?
Decision Maker in the room.
Whichever the setup of the team you need the ultimate decision-maker in the room. Avoid middle management unless there is decision making authority. Fast and deterministic decision making is part of 58’s secret sauce.
Facilitator is the boss.
Whatever the dynamics of the team outside the session, every 58 needs a facilitator. The facilitator is the gatekeeper of the session’s success. Every participant needs to respect its authority.
There’s no way back.
When a decision is made you can’t go back. Sure there will be things that will be subject to test after the session, but changing your mind in such an intense working session is counter productive and frustrating.
No Distractions.
Empty the team’s calendars, remove smartphone devices from clear sight, and create an interruption free workspace. Make sure that you pick a venue that protects your team from physical interruptions. Focus is really important for producing work with impact in such tight timeframes.
Key Challenge is written in Stone.
When beginning a session there’s one thing that cannot change, and this is the problem the team is set to solve. That’s why you need a strong pre work period to make sure you are solving the right problem.

Why it
works

One key Challenge to solve, with nothing to stand in your way.
We rarely get to work with laser focus with a team of like-minded people to answer one specific challenge, without any distractions for 3 days in a row. If you've ever been in such position, you know already the crazy volume of outcomes as a result of this.
Fierce crit over rounded talks.
Crit is what’s missing from most collaborative working sessions, and 58 brings it back by emphasizing on the trust we place on the subject matter experts vs collective thinking or rounded talks that try to avoid conflict inside teams.
Real Work vs Ideas.
The end goal of a 58 is to get tangible deliverables instead of ideas or just sketches. What we deliver should be ready for development or immediate execution rather than something you will need to work over and over again, before going to production.
The short time frame and the presence of a decision maker.
Makes any asynchronous communication unnecessary and as such makes the review and decision-making process faster and more efficient.
